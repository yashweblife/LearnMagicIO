Datatypes are these awesome thing that gets used by compilers to determine how much resource a program needs. They are very common in lower level languages like C and C++. These languages are also considered more efficient.

What are Datatypes

Think of a computer as a large chain of boxes,

When you make an integer, it occupies a certain number of boxes
Similarly, a string will occupy even more boxes
Say your code has 1 integer and 1 string, the memory of your computer would look like this:
You have a limit on how many boxes you can fill which depends on your computer. Properly written programs will make use of this space very efficiently.
There are 2 major types of data

Primary and Secondary

Primary

This kind of datatype ususally refers to homogenius units of data, like number, characters and booleans. A number can not be broken down into something else but a string can be broken down into characters,
Some Examples:

  • Integer
  • Characters
  • Booleans
  • Floats
  • Double

Secondary Datatypes

This kind of datatypes are more complex in what they can be, you can even "make your own". They are in some way a collection of the primary datatypes. A string is a collection of characters and an array is a collection of stuff.
Some Examples

  • Strings
  • Arrays
  • "Classes/Objects"
